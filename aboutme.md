I'm a PhD candidate in Behavior Analysis at Centro de Estudios e Investigaciones en Comportamiento (in english: Center of Studies and Research on Behavior, which is a strange name), in Guadalajara, México. 

I love all stuff related to Linux, Open Science and Free Knowledge. I like SciFi, especially Philip K. Dick, but my favourite novel is Crime and Punishment (Dostoevsky). For get things the fuck done, I listen to metal (noise, drone, stoner), to calm down I listen to metal (avant-garde, black, melodic death), to read I definetively listen to metal (progressive and whatever have a taste of jazz) and some blues or classic rock, or jazz. Our music is our soundtrack, so I have choose wisely. 

Academically, I'm interested in behaviorism (or the umbrella name of Behavior Analysis, which is more an approach in psychology to study behavior than a branch of science with it's own and consistent philosophy), cognitive sciences, statistics, math and learning (a more broad topic than just psychology that includes Reinforcement Learning and Machine Learning, advanced areas or research in engineering). Of course, I know almost nothing about that, but I have to look like I'm interesting.

I studied Pharmacy, Chemistry and Biology in college (here in Mexico there is a career with all of those topics, with the wordy name of Químico Farmacobiólogo) but I get bored too soon to persue more of the same, so I just turned on my interests into psychology. 

I like to commit heresies. Sometimes I think I'm near to be a pyrrhonian skeptic, but I like to run experiments anyway.
